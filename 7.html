  • 7. The Apalachian Mountains
The montane conifer forest of the Appalachian Mountains is dominated by spruce and fir, like the taiga. Balsam fir is the dominant species in the northern part, with Fraser fir replacing it in the southern Appalachian. Through the area, and occasionally even more common, is red spruce. Among the hardwood, both paper birch and yellow birch are common, especially on the edges of forests where the soil is wet. An understory species, the yew is often encountered growing with shrubs such as azaleas, rhododendrons (both of the genus Rhododendron) and numerous species of blueberries. Ferns are frequently found.1
